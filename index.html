Subtheme: [Speculation]

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. 1. How much profit are investors making from flipping houses in Boston?
  2. 2. Which neighbourhoods have the highest frequency of house flipping?
  3. 3. Out of the flipped properties, what are the investor types when buying and selling these properties? Do they prefer investing in a certain type of property?
  4. 4. How much profit do each type of investors make and how stable is the profit? What type of property type is the most profitable, and are they stable?

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

Key Insights: 1. House flipping is highly concentrated in high-end neighbourhoods like South End and Back Bay, while lower-income areas see significantly fewer flips. 2. Larger investors take on higher risk but have greater profit potential, while smaller investors and non-investors engage in more stable investments. 3. Multi-family residences (2RF, R3F) provide the most stable profits, whereas commercial properties have high but unpredictable returns. 4. Market trends suggest that external factors may have played a role in major profit spikes (2000, 2006, 2020) and dips (2010). Overall, the market for flipped properties stayed relatively stable during the 2000-2024 period in Boston. Further analysis could explore how economic shifts, interest rates, and zoning laws have influenced flipping trends over time.
